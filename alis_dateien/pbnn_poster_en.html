Physics-Informed Deep Learning for Particle Aggregation & Breakage

Chen, Wang, Meng & Luo  ·  Population Balance Neural Network (PBNN)  ·  forward & inverse problems

What it's about

Particles are everywhere in industry — crystals, droplets, ore in a mill — and they constantly clump together (aggregation) and break apart (breakage). Predicting how their size distribution evolves is the core challenge. Classical solvers handle the forward problem well but struggle with the inverse problem.

The model — Population Balance

The Population Balance Model (PBM) keeps a running count of how many particles of each size exist and how that distribution changes over time. It is a bookkeeping of four mechanisms — and forms a nonlinear integro-differential equation that is hard to solve.

dN/dt = + birth · aggregation − death · aggregation − death · breakage + birth · breakage

Rates are set by kernel functions: aggregation kernel β, breakage rate γ, and the daughter-fragment distribution α.

Research question

Can a physics-informed neural network solve both the forward and the inverse problems of the PBE — efficiently and physically consistently?

Method — PBNN

  • A neural network acts as a surrogate for the size distribution N(v,t)
  • The PBE is embedded directly in the loss function → unphysical predictions are penalized
  • Validated against analytical solutions and a real ball-mill experiment

Key takeaway

Embedding the physics directly in the loss function makes the network data-efficient and physically consistent — and, unlike classical solvers, it also handles inverse problems with noisy data.
